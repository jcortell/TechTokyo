TECH Tokyo

TECHNOLOGY EXCHANGE AND COLLABORATION HUB

BUILDING

Physical connections

Between researchers, entrepreneurs, academics, investors, and students. Via Tokyu Land Corporation's state-of-the-art center in Shibuya.

BUILDING

Virtual connections

Between researchers, entrepreneurs, academics, investors, and students. Via full-size real-time videoconference portals.

WHAT

The Technology Exchange and Collaboration Hub in Tokyo (TECH-Tokyo) is dedicated to connecting the world’s top 10 universities with leading Japanese corporations. Our aim is to foster robust collaborations through licensing, partnerships, investment into university spinoffs, and sponsored research, as well as to facilitate academic exchange and executive education.

HOW

Bringing faculty, researchers, and entrepreneurs—from some of the top 10 universities in the world—and Japanese corporations, entrepreneurs, investors, and universities, closer together. Exploring academic, partnering, and commercial opportunities through a multi-year program of presentations, lectures, workshops, networking, guest speakers, competitions and more.

WHERE

Attend lectures and presentations with simultaneous Japanese-English translation in Shibuya-Tokyo, and collaborate in real time with faculty, entrepreneurs, and researchers using a full-size real-time video portal.

MORE INFO

WHY

We believe that now, more than ever, it is critical for the world’s leading universities to unite, demonstrate resilience, and show solidarity in the face of the current political challenges and attacks on academic freedom. By coming together, we can strengthen our collective position and create a powerful, united front that supports academic innovation and entrepreneurship globally. Technology transfer and partnering is the engine that brings research and innovation to society to make the world a better place for all of us.

Activities

TECH-Tokyo is preparing a full calendar of activities, all year round, with speakers already confirmed from MIT, Stanford, Oxford University, Imperial College London, Cambridge University, and more.
